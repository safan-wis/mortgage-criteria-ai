## Customer

All directors and shareholders must be fully and properly identified as part of the mortgage application process.

Accurate information must be recorded on our application system, including confirmation of whether this is a face-to-face or non-face-to-face application.

If the director or shareholder doesn't pass our electronic verification checks, you'll need to certify and retain evidence of the identification documents.

The documents you can accept depend on whether you've met with the directors and shareholders in person, or remotely by telephone or video.

Here's a guide to understanding what documents you'll need.

For face-to-face verification, where the directors and shareholders are physically present and documentation is taken and verified, one document from the single identification list can be used to verify the directors and shareholders.

Single ID can't be used if the directors and shareholders aren't present when ID&V is collected. This includes video interviews. In this case, use the standard ID&V process.

These documents can be accepted in a former name with evidence of the name change, such as a marriage certificate, Deed Poll or Decree Absolute.

Standard ID&V must be followed when the directors and shareholders aren't present when ID&V is obtained, or the directors/shareholders doesn't have a single ID document.

The directors and shareholders will need to produce two separate documents. These must be originals and can only be used once in the process for either address verification or identification.

FCA registered firms must make sure any documentation relied upon to verify the directors and shareholders identity is copied, certified, and retained in accordance with money laundering regulations. We reserve the right to contact you and request copies of identity documents at any time during the life of the mortgage and for up to five years after cessation.

## Income

Affordability is assessed using the anticipated rental income from the property. We'll also assess personal income when making a lending decision, so it's important the directors and shareholders income is keyed accurately as it might affect the decision.

When doing this, we expect to see the directors and shareholders can afford their personal commitments, so we can be confident our lending decision is sustainable if the tenant stops paying or the property needs any maintenance or repairs. An application might be declined if our assessment shows there's not enough disposable income to support the borrowing and the directors and shareholders personal commitments.

In our rental cover calculation, we want the directors and shareholders to have more rent coming in than the normal mortgage payment, so the situations mentioned earlier can be covered.

We'll also take the additional cost of increased tax liability for directors or shareholders into account.

We understand personal income might not fully reflect the overall financial picture for the directors and shareholders. They could have other assets and savings, for example. We also know the buy to let might be in a single name, but personal financial commitments could be held jointly with someone else.

Foreign currency income is any income other than sterling received by directors or shareholders. Foreign currency income paid into a UK bank account and transferred to sterling is still considered to be foreign currency.

Foreign currency income won't be accepted for new mortgage or remortgage applications for new and existing directors or shareholders, including porting, transfers of mortgaged property (TOMP), and further advances.

The only exceptions to this are further advance applications for essential repairs to the mortgaged property and product transfers.

If your directors or shareholders are classed as employed, they'll need to have been in their current role for at least three months.

If your directors or shareholders are classed as self-employed, we'll need at least two years' worth of trading figures.

We'll consider applications from these directors and shareholders if they have continuous employment of 12 months or more with six months of the contract remaining, or the directors and shareholders have two years' continuous service in the same type of employment. Evidence of their employment record might be needed.

For IT contractors on any income and other contractors whose income is more than  500 per day or  75,000 per annum, whether employed or self-employed, we accept the gross value of the contract as evidence of income. All directors and shareholders must have a current continuous employment of 12 months or more with six months of the contract remaining or two years continuous service in the same type of employment.

The directors and shareholders should be treated as self-employed if they have more than one contract, or where they've set up a limited company and employ other contractors.

For these directors and shareholders, they'll need to provide an average of their latest 3 months income. They should be treated as self-employed if they pay their own tax or sub-contract to more than one company.

Applications from directors and shareholders employed on a zero-hours contract will be considered. We'll only take income from the total of the last 12 calendar months into account income. Directors and shareholders will have to verify their income.

Professional sports people will have to confirm they've either been in continuous employment for 12 months or more with six months of the contract remaining, or two years' continuous service as at the date of application in the same type of employment.

It's essential to verify these directors and shareholders will be able to meet their monthly repayments as they near the end of their career or if their career's ended early because of injury.

A dependant is anyone who relies on your directors or shareholders financially. They won't be included on the mortgage or contribute to the monthly payments.

Child and adult dependants need to be captured separately.

These are any children under 18 who the directors or shareholders are the primary carer for. You should also include any children they're expecting or in the process of adopting. Foster children or any children who have an income they can fully support themselves with don't need to be included.

Child maintenance must be keyed as an outgoing commitment, but the child doesn't need to be included as a dependant.

An adult dependant is someone not included in the mortgage but is financially reliant on the directors or shareholders. This could be a spouse/partner, elderly relative, or any grown-up children in full-time education or unemployed.

If the adult dependant doesn't live at the property all of the time, still key this in the adult dependant section.

Properties in buildings five storeys or higher no longer need an External Wall System (EWS1) form in order to progress an application. We'll also consider applications on properties where cladding remediation work is needed but not yet completed.

Will still need an EWS1 form regardless of height. Directors or shareholders will have to source the EWS1 form themselves.
